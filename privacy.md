_Last updated: 2026-06-29_

xrk-autosync ("the app") is a desktop application that syncs your AiM racing
telemetry files (`.xrk`/`.xrz`) to your own Google Drive and mirrors them across
your own computers. This policy explains what happens to your data. The short
version: **the app runs entirely on your computer and in your own Google Drive —
the developer operates no server and never receives, stores, or has access to
your data.**

## What we collect

Nothing. xrk-autosync has no backend server, no analytics, no user accounts, and
no reporting back to the developer. We do not collect, transmit, or store any
personal information or any of your files.

## Your Google account and Google Drive

When you sign in, you grant the app access using Google's **`drive.file`**
permission — the narrowest Drive permission available. With it, the app can see
and manage **only the files it creates itself** in your Drive (the telemetry it
uploads and the folders it makes). It **cannot** read or access any of your other
Google Drive files.

The app uses this access solely to:

- upload your telemetry files to your Drive, organized by track / date / driver; and
- download those same files to your other computers so your library stays in sync.

Your Google sign-in token is stored **locally on your own computer**
(`%LOCALAPPDATA%\xrk-autosync\`) and is never sent to the developer or anyone else.

## Where your data lives

- Your telemetry files stay on your computer and in **your own** Google Drive.
- The developer has no server and keeps no copy of your files, account, or token.
- The only third party involved is Google (your own Drive). Google's handling of
  your data is governed by Google's own Privacy Policy.

## Revoking access

You can revoke the app's access to your Google Drive at any time at
<https://myaccount.google.com/permissions>. You may also delete the locally
cached token folder (`%LOCALAPPDATA%\xrk-autosync\`).

## Children's privacy

The app is not directed to children and collects no information from anyone.
